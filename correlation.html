Portfolio Implications

Concentration Risk

  • Correlated clusters move together. Holding multiple names from the same cluster amplifies drawdowns — a sector-wide selloff hits all positions simultaneously.
  • High correlation pairs (|r| > 0.50) should not both be held at full size. If SMCI and another tech name correlate at 0.70, the effective position is ~1.7x a single name in a correlated move.
  • Diversifier pairs (|r| < 0.15) are the best portfolio offsets. Combining one meme/social play with a low-corr name reduces portfolio-level volatility without sacrificing expected return.
  • Rule of thumb: if average pairwise correlation exceeds 0.40, the portfolio behaves like a concentrated bet on a single factor (retail sentiment). Trim correlated positions or add hedges.
