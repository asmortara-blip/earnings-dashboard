XVI. SWOT Analysis

The SWOT framework provides a structured assessment of the strategy's competitive position. This analysis informs strategic decisions regarding resource allocation, risk management priorities, and the timeline for Phase 4 deployment. Each quadrant synthesizes quantitative evidence from the trading record with qualitative assessment of the operational infrastructure.

Strengths

  • Win rate of 64.3% significantly exceeds the 55% minimum gate threshold, providing a substantial margin of safety
  • W/L ratio of 2.08x demonstrates asymmetric payoff profile with larger average wins than losses
  • Diversified catalyst coverage across analyst upgrades, earnings, social velocity spikes, and technical breakouts
  • Automated 220+ daemon infrastructure ensures 24/7 signal monitoring with minimal human intervention
  • Multi-layer signal validation (entry checklist, quality gate, risk gater) reduces false positive rate
  • Pyramid entry structure (50%/30%/20% tranches) provides natural cost averaging and conviction scaling
  • Proven Phase 2 shadow-mode validation with 57 trades matching backtest expectations

Weaknesses

  • SL (Sentiment Level) factor carries 15% weight but contributes zero information due to StockTwits 403 error
  • Limited short-side trade volume — long bias may underperform in bearish market regimes
  • All paper trades executed during a single macro regime (risk_on), leaving risk_off performance unvalidated
  • High concentration in top 5 tickers creates single-name dependency risk
  • Reddit API rate limits and ApeWisdom polling frequency create potential data latency of up to 15 minutes
  • No direct market data feed — reliance on yfinance for price data introduces execution timing risk
  • Benzinga WebSocket trigger still in dry-run mode pending API key procurement

Opportunities

  • Phase 4 live trading with real capital, leveraging validated paper trading track record
  • SV/SL weight redistribution: reallocating 15% SL weight to SV (40%) and SA (18%) could improve score accuracy
  • New trigger type expansion: Discord sentiment, Telegram groups, TikTok viral mentions
  • Options structure recommendations (rs_options_recommender.py) could enhance risk-adjusted returns
  • Cross-asset signals: BTC/gold/bond correlation data could improve regime detection accuracy
  • ML model enhancement: current logistic regression could be upgraded to gradient-boosted trees
  • International expansion: applying the RS model to European or Asian meme stock equivalents
  • Intraday stat-arb pairs trading to generate returns during low-signal periods

Threats

  • Regime change risk: a sustained shift to risk_off (VIX >28, SPY downtrend) could significantly impair returns
  • Alpha decay: signal edge deteriorates over time as more participants adopt similar social-sentiment strategies
  • Liquidity constraints: meme stock liquidity can evaporate during panic selling, widening bid-ask spreads
  • Regulatory risk: SEC scrutiny of retail trading patterns could alter market microstructure
  • Data source disruption: ApeWisdom API changes, Reddit API pricing, or StockTwits permanent blocking
  • Crowding risk: increased retail participation in the same names could front-run or dilute signal alpha
  • Technology risk: launchd daemon failures, database corruption, or network outages during critical trading windows

XVII. Factor Attribution

Factor attribution decomposes the RS composite score into its five constituent components, measuring each factor's average contribution to winning versus losing trades. The efficiency ratio (average factor value in wins divided by overall average) indicates how effectively each component discriminates between profitable and unprofitable setups. Factors with efficiency ratios significantly above 1.0 are pulling their weight; those below 0.8 may be adding noise rather than signal.

The RS score formula: RS = SV x 0.35 + TQ x 0.20 + SL x 0.15 + VC x 0.15 + SA x 0.15, plus convergence bonuses, sector dampeners, and macro regime multipliers.

XXV. Alpha Decay Analysis

Alpha decay measures the rate at which the strategy's informational edge diminishes after signal generation. In a retail sentiment momentum context, the initial catalyst (social media spike, analyst upgrade, gap) creates an information asymmetry that the market progressively prices in. The exponential decay model alpha(t) = A * exp(-lambda * t) + C estimates the half-life of this edge.

Alpha decay data not available. Run rs_alpha_decay.py to fit the exponential decay curve and determine optimal holding periods by window, score bucket, and trigger type.

Understanding alpha decay is essential for calibrating hold times, trailing stop distances, and the decision to take partial profits. Trades held significantly beyond the half-life are effectively gambling on noise rather than exploiting a genuine informational advantage. The hold time analysis in Section XX should be interpreted in conjunction with these decay estimates.

XXVIII. Appendix B: Methodology

B.1 RS Composite Score Formula

The Retail Sentiment (RS) composite score is a weighted multi-factor model designed to quantify the probability that a given ticker will experience a significant short-term price move driven by crowd behavior. The formula is:

RS = (SV x 0.35 + TQ x 0.20 + SL x 0.15 + VC x 0.15 + SA x 0.15) x WC_multiplier x RES_tier_multiplier
+ convergence_bonus + large_print_TQ_boost
x sector_ETF_dampener x macro_dampener

Where each component is scored on a 0-100 scale:

  • SV (Sentiment Velocity, 35%): Measures the rate of change in social media mentions via ApeWisdom rank/mention ratio. A 50x surge maps to SV=95; a 10x surge to SV=75; a 2x surge to SV=45.
  • TQ (Trigger Quality, 20%): Pre-assigned score for each catalyst type (e.g., analyst_upgrade_bold=90, influencer_post_high=80, options_call_surge=75). Augmented by +5 for large Alpaca block trades >=10,000 shares.
  • SL (Sentiment Level, 15%): 30-minute rolling StockTwits sentiment delta. Currently returning default 30.0 for all signals due to API access issues (403 response).
  • VC (Volume Confirmation, 15%): For W2 (intraday) signals, RVOL relative to 20-day average. For W1 (pre-market) signals, gap magnitude as a proxy for volume interest.
  • SA (Short Amplifier, 15%): Composite of SI% x 2.5 + SI velocity bonus + borrow rate bonus + FINRA short-volume bonus. High SA values indicate squeeze potential.

B.2 Data Sources

The strategy ingests data from the following sources, each feeding specific components of the RS scoring model:

  • ApeWisdom: Reddit WSB/stocks mention counts and rank data, polled every 15 minutes. Primary input for SV calculation.
  • Reddit (PRAW or JSON API): Supplementary social velocity data from r/wallstreetbets and r/stocks subreddits.
  • StockTwits: Sentiment level data (currently blocked, returning 403).
  • yfinance: Real-time and historical price/volume data for RVOL calculation, gap scanning, and P&L tracking.
  • Alpaca Markets: WebSocket block trade detection (>=10,000 shares or $500k+), RVOL baselines, and paper trading execution.
  • FINRA: CNMS short-sale volume data, processed daily at 7:00 AM ET.
  • Finviz: Short interest percentage and borrow rate data, processed weekly.
  • Yahoo Finance RSS: News headline scanner for catalyst detection, polled every 3 minutes.
  • Google Trends: Search velocity for ticker symbols, providing a leading indicator with 4-hour cache.
  • Options chain (yfinance): Call volume ratios, OTM clustering, IV spikes, and OI day-over-day changes.

B.3 Backtesting Methodology

The Phase 1 backtest (rs_backtest.py, v5 FINAL) was conducted on 56 trades across 30 tickers using both curated historical events and auto-detected RVOL signals. The backtest used walk-forward validation with TimeSeriesSplit to prevent look-ahead bias. Trade simulation assumed market-on-open execution for W1 signals and 15-minute delayed entry for W2 signals, with realistic slippage estimates based on the Almgren-Chriss market impact model.

B.4 Paper Trading Validation

Phase 2 shadow-mode monitoring ran rs_monitor.py with the --shadow flag, logging all signals without executing paper trades. Phase 3 removed the shadow flag and enabled paper trading with live price feeds via yfinance. Entry prices are captured at signal_time + 15 minutes (or market open for pre-market signals). Exit conditions include: ATR trailing stop, hard -5% stop-loss, SV reversal, and time-based boundaries. All trades are recorded in the rs_monitor.db SQLite database.

B.5 Phase Gate Criteria

The Phase 4 deployment gate requires simultaneous satisfaction of five hard gates: (1) >= 50 paper trades, (2) >= 55% win rate, (3) >= 1.5x win-to-loss ratio, (4) >= -15% maximum drawdown, and (5) > 1.0 annualized Sharpe ratio. Additionally, 8 soft gates are evaluated by rs_phase4_readiness.py, including regime diversification, ticker coverage breadth, and execution quality metrics.
